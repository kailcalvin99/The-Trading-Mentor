## Video 8: Analytics — Reading Your Report Card

**Duration:** ~4–5 minutes
**Title Card:** "Analytics — Reading Your Report Card"

---

**[SCENE: The Analytics page loads. Stat cards appear at the top — Win Rate, Total Trades, Profit Factor, Average Risk.]**

**NARRATION:**

Numbers don't lie. And the Analytics page is where your numbers live.

Think of this as your trading report card. It takes every trade you've logged and turns it into charts, scores, and insights. Let me show you how to read it.

**[SCENE: Highlight the four stat cards at the top of the page.]**

Right at the top, you see four numbers.

Win Rate — this is how often you win. If you took ten trades and won six, your win rate is 60%. Above 50% is good. Below 50% means you're losing more than you're winning.

Total Trades — how many trades you've completed. This number should grow slowly. If it's growing fast, you might be overtrading.

Profit Factor — this is your wins divided by your losses, measured in risk. If your profit factor is above 1.0, you're making money. If it's below 1.0, you're losing money. Simple.

Average Risk — how much you risked per trade on average. If this number is above 2%, you're risking too much. Keep it at 1% or below.

**[SCENE: Trap #1 — Show a trader taking 10+ trades in a day. The Smart Journal shows a long list of trades.]**

Trap number one: Overtrading.

This is the most common mistake new traders make. You take one trade, it doesn't go your way, so you take another. And another. And another. Before you know it, you've taken 8 trades in a day, you're down 4%, and you feel sick.

How the app protects you: Your Smart Journal logs every trade. When you look back at your Analytics, you can see exactly how many trades you took each day. If you see a pattern of 5+ trades per day, that's a red flag. The Daily Planner also has a "Max Trades" field — set it to 2 and hold yourself to it.

**[SCENE: Trap #2 — Show the CoolDown Overlay activating. The screen is blocked by the cool-down timer.]**

Trap number two: Revenge trading.

You took a loss. Now you're angry. You want your money back. So you take a bigger trade, throw out your rules, and try to "get even" with the market.

How the app protects you: The CoolDown Overlay. When you hit a certain number of consecutive losses — the default is 2 in a row — the app locks you out. A full-screen timer appears. You cannot trade until the timer runs out. It might feel frustrating in the moment, but it is saving your account.

The cool-down gives you time to breathe, review your trades, and come back with a clear head. Use that time wisely. Go for a walk. Study a lesson. Do anything except stare at the chart.

**[SCENE: Trap #3 — Show the "Routine Required" lockout on the Smart Journal page.]**

Trap number three: Skipping the morning routine.

You wake up late, the market is already moving, and you think, "I'll just skip the checklist today and jump right in." Bad idea.

How the app protects you: The Discipline Gate. If you haven't completed your Morning Routine in the Daily Planner, the Smart Journal locks you out. You literally cannot log a trade until the routine is done. This forces you to slow down and prepare before you act.

The routine only takes a few minutes. But those few minutes are the difference between a planned trade and a panic trade.

**[SCENE: Trap #4 — Show the Risk Shield with a position size that's too large. The drawdown gauge is in the red zone.]**

Trap number four: Ignoring the Position Size Calculator.

You think the setup is perfect. You're 100% sure it's going to work. So you size up — you trade 5 contracts instead of 1. And when the trade goes against you, your drawdown gauge jumps into the red.

How the app protects you: The Risk Shield shows you exactly what happens when you over-size. The drawdown gauges don't lie. If you traded too big and lost, the gauge moves — fast. After a few painful experiences, you'll learn to trust the calculator and trade the size it tells you.

The calculator says 1 contract? Trade 1 contract. Not 2. Not 3. One. The math is non-negotiable.

Trap number five: Trading outside of kill zones.

You're bored. The London session is over. NY Open is done. But you're still watching charts at 2 PM, looking for something — anything — to trade. And you take a mediocre setup because you're restless.

How the app protects you: The Analytics page shows your Kill Zone chart — your win rate broken down by the hour. If most of your losses happen at 1 PM or 3 PM, the chart makes it obvious. You can literally see when you should and shouldn't be trading.

The highest-probability trades happen during specific windows: London Open, NY Open, and Silver Bullet. Outside those windows, the odds drop. The data proves it.
